The Praxis Network: Equipping humanities graduate and undergraduate students for scholarship and future careers through innovative methodological training and research support.

The Praxis Network is a suite of allied but differently-inflected initiatives that are rethinking the modes of humanities education. Each of the programs featured here has been developed within a unique ecosystem of disciplinary expectations, institutional needs, resources, leadership, and challenges. About

This informal network was developed with funding from the Andrew W. Mellon Foundation as part of the Scholarly Communication Institute's 2012-2013 work stream on rethinking graduate education. The collection of programs highlights several possible responses to the emerging literacies necessary in humanities disciplines, as well as the changing post-graduate career opportunities for students. It complements a survey on perceptions of career preparation among humanities graduate students and their employers, as well as a series of meetings that SCI is convening in partnership with the Consortium of Humanities Centers and Institutes (CHCI) and centerNet, an international network of digital humanities centers.
